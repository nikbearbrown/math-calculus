# Figure briefs — Chapter 1: Functions and Graphs

## Figure 1.1 (optional): Three earthquakes on a logarithmic axis

**Placement:** §1.1, after the first paragraph.

**Description:** Bar chart with three bars showing M=7.3, M=8.2, M=9.0 on a *linear* magnitude scale (looks like three close-together bars). Below it, the same three quakes on a logarithmic *amplitude* scale showing the actual physical separation — the 9.0 bar towers over the 7.3 bar.

**Pedagogical purpose:** Make the chapter's central reveal visual — the magnitudes look close because the scale is logarithmic.

**Style notes:** Two-panel chart. Same horizontal placement of bars in both panels. Vertical axis labeled differently in each.

## Figure 1.2 (optional): Function as a mapping diagram

**Placement:** §1.2.

**Description:** Two ovals (domain $D$, range $R$) with arrows from each $x \in D$ to its image $f(x) \in R$. Beside it, a "not a function" version where one $x$ maps to two outputs.

**Pedagogical purpose:** Illustrate the "exactly one output" rule.

## Figure 1.3 (optional): Function families gallery

**Placement:** §1.3.

**Description:** Six small graphs in a 2×3 grid: linear, quadratic, cubic, rational ($1/x$), $\sqrt{x}$, exponential. Each labeled with formula and family.

**Pedagogical purpose:** Quick visual reference for the standard family shapes.

## Figure 1.4 (optional): The unit circle

**Placement:** §1.4.

**Description:** Unit circle in the $(x,y)$-plane, with reference angles $\pi/6, \pi/4, \pi/3, \pi/2, 2\pi/3, 3\pi/4, 5\pi/6, \pi$ etc. labeled. Coordinates of each labeled angle shown (e.g., $(\sqrt{3}/2, 1/2)$ at $\pi/6$).

**Pedagogical purpose:** The single most-referenced figure in trigonometry. Worth committing to memory.

**Style notes:** Standard textbook unit-circle diagram. Color-code quadrants.

## Figure 1.5 (optional): Inverse-function symmetry

**Placement:** §1.5.

**Description:** Graph of $f(x) = e^x$ and $f^{-1}(x) = \ln x$ on the same axes, with the line $y = x$ as a dashed reflection axis.

**Pedagogical purpose:** The "graphs of $f$ and $f^{-1}$ are mirror images across $y = x$" claim deserves a picture.

## Figure 1.6 (optional): Logistic growth formula

**Placement:** §1.6.

**Description:** Graph of $P(t) = P_0 K e^{rt} / [K + P_0(e^{rt} - 1)]$ with carrying capacity $K$ shown as horizontal asymptote, exponential phase highlighted at low $t$, and inflection point labeled.

**Pedagogical purpose:** Show how the chapter's full machinery integrates into a single curve students will meet again in differential-equations work.
