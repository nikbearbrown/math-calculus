# Pantry — Chapter 5: Integration
## Scenes
- Cyclist with continuously varying speed wanting total distance — §5.1, §5.7.
## Worked examples
- $\int_0^4 x^2 \, dx = 64/3$ — Riemann sum vs FTC. §5.2-5.3.
- Substitution $\int 2x \cos(x^2) dx = \sin(x^2) + C$. §5.5.
- Definite-integral substitution $\int_0^1 (3x^2+1)\sqrt{x^3+x+1} \, dx$. §5.5.
- Cyclist with sinusoidal velocity → 18 miles. §5.7.
- Cyclist with hill (no closed form) → numerical ≈ 16.94 mi. §5.7.
## Trade-offs named
- FTC buys computability at cost of requiring antiderivative; no closed form requires numerical methods. §5.3.
- Substitution buys composition handling at cost of recognizing the right $u$. §5.5.
## Reusable terms
Riemann sum; left/right/midpoint endpoint; definite integral; indefinite integral; antiderivative; constant of integration; Fundamental Theorem of Calculus (Parts 1 and 2); substitution; linearity; additivity; reversed limits; comparison.
