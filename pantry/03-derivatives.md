# Pantry — Chapter 3: Derivatives
## Scenes
- Hennessey Venom GT 0-200 mph in 14.51 seconds, top speed 270.49 mph (2014). §3.1.
## Worked examples
- $f(x) = x^2$ derivative via limit definition → $2x$. §3.2.
- $(3x+1)/(x^2+1)$ via quotient rule. §3.4.
- $(x^2+3)^5$ via chain rule. §3.4.
- $v(t) = v_\text{max}(1 - e^{-t/\tau})$ giving $a(t) = (v_\text{max}/\tau)e^{-t/\tau}$. §3.7.
## Trade-offs named
- Limit definition is canonical at cost of slowness; rules are fast at cost of memorization. §3.2-3.4.
- Differentiability stronger than continuity at cost of excluding corners and vertical tangents. §3.3.
