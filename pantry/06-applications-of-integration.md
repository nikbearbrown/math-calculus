# Pantry — Chapter 6: Applications of Integration
## Scenes
- Wine bottle as solid of revolution — §6.1, §6.7. Reusable for any volume-of-revolution context.
## Worked examples
- Area between $y=x^2$ and $y=x+2$ → 9/2. §6.2.
- Volume of paraboloid from $\sqrt{x}$ rotated → $8\pi$. §6.3.
- $y=x^2$ rotated around y-axis (shell) → $8\pi$. §6.4.
- Arc length of $(2/3)x^{3/2}$ from 0 to 3 → 14/3. §6.5.
- Spring work $k=4, x=0.5$ → 0.5 J. §6.6.
- Wine bottle three-piece volume → ~1.17 L. §6.7.
## Trade-offs named
- Disk vs shell methods: choose by axis-orientation relative to slicing direction. §6.4.
- Most arc-length integrals have no closed form. §6.5.
## Reusable terms
Solid of revolution; disk method; washer method; shell method; arc length; work (variable force); pumping problem; Hooke's law; slice-identify-integrate.
