# Pantry — Chapter 1: Functions and Graphs

## Scenes

- **Three earthquakes — Haiti 2010 (M 7.3), Japan 2011 (M 9.0), Chile 2014 (M 8.2)** — used in §1.1 cold open. Logarithmic-scale comparison: 10^(9-7.3) ≈ 50× amplitude ratio between Japan and Haiti, ~350× energy ratio. Reusable in any chapter on logarithmic scales, signal compression, or natural-disaster magnitude.

## Analogies

- **The Richter scale as "compression that hides a multiplicative gap"** — explains logarithmic scales. Limit: works only for unfamiliar audiences; the trade-off (compactness vs naive comparability) needs to be named.
- **Composition as "first one rule, then another"** — emphasizes order matters. Limit: breaks down for commuting compositions like $f(x) = x^2$ and $g(x) = -x$.

## Etymologies

- **Transcendental** — from Latin *trans-* (across) + *scandere* (to climb). Functions that "climb beyond" what algebra can construct.
- **Logarithm** — from Greek *logos* (ratio, word) + *arithmos* (number). Originally "ratio number" — a number that captures multiplicative structure.
- **Sine** — from Latin *sinus* (curve, fold), itself a Latin translation of an Arabic transliteration of the Sanskrit *jya* (bowstring).

## Trade-offs named

- **Polynomials buy unrestricted domain at the cost of unbounded behavior at infinity.** §1.3.
- **Logarithmic scales buy compact representation at the cost of misleading naive comparison.** §1.5, §1.1.
- **Radian measure buys clean derivative formulas at the cost of departing from everyday degree intuition.** §1.4.
- **Restriction of domain buys an inverse at the cost of losing some original outputs.** §1.5.

## Scale shifts used

- **Three-digit difference (8.2 vs 9.0) → 350-fold energy ratio.** §1.1. From comparable-looking numbers to vastly disparate physical magnitudes.
- **Logistic-growth formula combining every function family in one expression.** §1.6. From individual function templates to a working synthesis.

## Worked examples

- **Earthquake magnitude comparison** — Japan 9.0 vs Haiti 7.3 → 50× amplitude, 350× energy.
- **Function composition $f(x) = x^2, g(x) = x+1$** — produces $(f \circ g)(x) = x^2 + 2x + 1$ and $(g \circ f)(x) = x^2 + 1$. Different.
- **Inverse of $f(x) = x^3 + 4$** = $\sqrt[3]{x - 4}$.
- **Trig at $7\pi/6$** — third quadrant, reference angle $\pi/6$, sign negative, value $-1/2$.
